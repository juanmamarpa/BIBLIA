# BIBLIA Glossary

**Version:** 0.1.0-draft

## Canon

The authoritative, version-controlled collection of durable project knowledge.

## Durable knowledge

Information that must survive conversations, people, tools, and AI models because it is needed to understand, verify, operate, evolve, or recover the project.

## Knowledge artifact

A structured, identifiable unit of durable knowledge managed by BIBLIA.

## Preservation

The complete process of collecting, classifying, reconciling, writing, validating, publishing, and remotely verifying durable knowledge.

## Consolidation

Integration of new or changed knowledge into its canonical destination without uncontrolled duplication or loss of material context.

## Traceability

The ability to follow knowledge from its source through decisions, revisions, relationships, implementation, evidence, and supersession.

## Supersession

The explicit replacement of an artifact or decision by a newer one while preserving the prior state and the reason for change.

## Evidence

Information that supports, refutes, or qualifies a claim or hypothesis.

## Hypothesis

A provisional proposition that has not yet been established as fact.

## Unknown

A relevant gap whose answer is not currently known or supported.

## Canon integrity

The condition in which identifiers, metadata, relationships, lifecycle states, contradictions, and publication records satisfy BIBLIA rules.

## Recovery

Continuation or reconstruction of a project using the Canon without requiring historical conversations or individual memory.

## Verified remote state

Repository content confirmed to exist in the configured remote after publication. It is the authoritative preserved state.
